Hi! My name is Thannia, I was born in 1991 and started working as a software engineer in 2014 (you do the math), however,
I've been drawing comics since I was a kid. 
I grew up in the mexican state of Sonora, right next to Arizona, where there is... not much going on. 

At some point in my life I realized that fantasy always took place in the forest, and I don't think I even visited a forest until I was in my 20s.
If by any chance a fantastic world or story had a desert it was always the "camels, dunes of sand and people with headscarves" kind of desert
and never a *cactus* kind of desert like the one I knew...

Did you know that *alebrijes* were invented by some dude in the 40s that dreamt about them and now they're regarded part of mexican culture and folklore? 
That made me realize that tradition has to start at some point. We see a lot of fantasy, with knights and witches, as taking place in the past
when these things existed but I'm sure there was a time when that was regular life. 

So I wanted to do fantasy that took place in the desert, the western kind of desert. And since things can start at any point there is no need for it
to take part in some kind of distant past, this world can have computers and stuff. That's where Thorns of a Dragon comes from. Also, I love dragons, so 
it had to have dragons as well.

This is pretty much me giving me permission to do whatever I want, what I like best, without worrying if it's cringe or original. 

I must confess that even tho it's all loosely based on things that exist where I'm from, I did 0 research, everything is made up there is no 
direct inspiration from existing cultures so I will as well apologize if somethings comes across as ignorant of offensive. 

The comic is entirely hand-drawn, mostly because that's what's easiest / fastest for me. I want to take away the barriers that would keep me 
from finishing it and if I color it I might get lazy. There is a little bit of digital retouch after I scan to make it clearer and maybe 
remove an odd line here and there.

All translations are also done by me, I try to keep the intention as true as possible. 

And yes, I know "Thorns of a Dragon" doesn't translate directly to "Fragmentos de Dragón", I chose what sounded best on each language.
